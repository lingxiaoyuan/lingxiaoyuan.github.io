I'm a forth year Ph.D. student in Boston University, Mechanical Engineering Department. I'm working on interdisciplinary subjects with skills and experience in applying machine learning methods to engineering problems. My current research project is about interpretable machine learning methods, e.g., physics-informed models, symbolic regression, genetic algorithms and causal inference. My previous projects include using generative models to search for new materials with novel functionality, improving out-of-distribution generalization accuracy through causal learning. I enjoy new challenges in life and work and I’m always trying to explore and learn new things. Apart from research and study, I like hiking, playing tennis, board games, and reading.
